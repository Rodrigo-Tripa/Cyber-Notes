# Operating System Security

## Overview

Operating system security consists of the mechanisms used to protect systems, users, applications, and data from unauthorized access, malware, and other cyber threats. Modern operating systems implement multiple layers of defense to maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) of system resources.

---

## Security Mechanisms

Common security mechanisms include:

- User Accounts
- Authentication
- Authorization
- File Permissions
- Firewalls
- Antivirus / Endpoint Protection
- Encryption
- Backups
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- System Updates

These mechanisms work together to reduce the likelihood and impact of security incidents.

---

## Principle of Least Privilege

The Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP) states that users, applications, and services should only receive the minimum permissions required to perform their intended tasks.

Restricting privileges reduces the attack surface and limits the damage that can occur if an account or application is compromised.

---

## Patch Management

Operating system vendors regularly release updates to fix vulnerabilities, improve stability, and introduce new features.

Keeping systems updated is one of the most effective ways to protect against known security vulnerabilities and malware.

---

## Endpoint Protection

Endpoint protection solutions monitor devices for malicious activity and help prevent, detect, and respond to cyber threats.

Modern operating systems often include built-in antivirus, anti-malware, firewall, and exploit protection features.

---

## Disk Encryption

Disk encryption protects data stored on storage devices by making it unreadable without the correct authentication credentials or encryption keys.

This helps protect sensitive information if a device is lost or stolen.

---

## System Recovery

Operating systems include recovery mechanisms such as backups, restore points, and volume snapshots to recover from hardware failures, accidental deletion, or malware infections.

Regular backups remain one of the most important security practices.

---

## Defense in Depth

Operating system security relies on multiple independent layers of protection rather than a single security control.

If one defensive layer fails, additional mechanisms continue protecting the system.

---

## Goal

The goal of operating system security is to protect system resources, reduce the attack surface, and ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.
