## Overview

The defender mindset focuses on protecting systems by anticipating attacks, reducing risk, and responding effectively when incidents occur. Defenders assume that attacks are inevitable and concentrate on minimizing their impact through preparation, monitoring, and continuous improvement.

## Core Principles

- Assume compromise.
- Protect the most critical assets first.
- Reduce the attack surface.
- Monitor continuously.
- Respond quickly to security incidents.
- Learn from every incident.

## Visibility

Effective defense depends on visibility. Defenders must understand what systems, users, applications, and network activity exist within their environment in order to detect abnormal behavior and investigate potential threats.

## Defense in Depth

No single security control is sufficient. Defensive security relies on multiple layers of protection such as firewalls, access controls, endpoint protection, encryption, logging, and monitoring. If one control fails, others continue to reduce the likelihood of a successful attack.

## Goal

The objective of the defender mindset is to minimize risk by preventing attacks whenever possible, detecting malicious activity quickly, responding effectively to incidents, and continuously improving the organization's security posture.
