## Overview

The attacker mindset is the ability to think like an adversary by continuously looking for weaknesses that can be exploited. Rather than assuming a system is secure, attackers assume that every system has flaws and focus on discovering ways to abuse them.

## Core Principles

- Think like an adversary.
- Never trust assumptions.
- Gather as much information as possible.
- Look for the weakest link.
- Chain multiple small weaknesses into a larger attack.
- Stay curious and adapt to new situations.

## Attack Surface

Attackers begin by identifying the attack surface, which includes every possible entry point into a system. This may include exposed services, web applications, user accounts, software vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, or even human factors such as social engineering.

## Enumeration First

Successful attacks rarely begin with exploitation. They begin with reconnaissance and enumeration to collect information about the target, understand its environment, and identify potential attack vectors before attempting to exploit them.

## Goal

The objective of adopting an attacker mindset is to understand how adversaries think so vulnerabilities can be identified and mitigated before they are exploited by malicious actors.
