## Overview

Cybersecurity offers a wide range of career paths focused on protecting, testing, and improving computer systems. Roles are generally divided into offensive, defensive, engineering, and research disciplines.

## Offensive Security

- Penetration Tester
- Red Team Operator
- Security Researcher

Focuses on finding and exploiting vulnerabilities before attackers do.

## Defensive Security

- SOC Analyst
- Incident Responder
- Threat Hunter
- Malware Analyst

Focuses on monitoring systems, detecting threats, investigating incidents, and protecting organizations.

## Engineering

- Security Engineer
- Security Architect

Focuses on designing, implementing, and maintaining secure infrastructure.

## Research

- Security Researcher
- Vulnerability Researcher
- Malware Researcher

Focuses on discovering and analyzing vulnerabilities, studying malware, reverse engineering software, and researching new attack techniques to improve cybersecurity knowledge and develop more effective defenses.

## Skills

Most cybersecurity careers require knowledge of:

- Operating Systems
- Networking
- Programming
- Security Principles
- Problem Solving
- Continuous Learning

## Career Progression

Cybersecurity professionals often specialize over time, moving from general technical roles into areas such as penetration testing, incident response, cloud security, malware analysis, digital forensics, or security engineering.
